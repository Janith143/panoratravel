## The Golden Rule: Layering

The key to packing for Sri Lanka is versatility. Since the temperature changes drastically depending on your altitude, you want lightweight, breathable fabrics (like linen and cotton) for the coast and sturdy layers for the mountains.

## üõï The "Temple Kit"

Sri Lanka takes its religious sites very seriously. When visiting Buddhist and Hindu temples (like the Temple of the Tooth in Kandy or the Dambulla Cave Temples), you **must** adhere to the dress code.

**What you need:**
1. **Shoulders MUST be covered:** No tank tops or spaghetti straps. Bring a large, lightweight scarf or sarong you can throw over your shoulders.
2. **Knees MUST be covered:** Shorts (for both men and women) are often not allowed. Pack loose, long pants or a long skirt.
3. **Easy-to-remove shoes:** You cannot wear shoes inside most shrines or temples. Slip-on sandals or flip-flops will save you a lot of hassle.
4. **Socks:** This sounds weird, but the stone floors outside the temples get *extremely* hot under the midday sun. Pack an old pair of socks to protect your feet!

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Some temples are stricter than others. Even if you see tourists wearing shorts, you may be denied entry. Always carry a wrap or scarf in your day bag just in case.

## üêò Safari Gear

If your itinerary includes Yala, Udawalawe, or Minneriya National Park, remember that safaris take place in open-air jeeps. It gets dusty, hot, and bumpy!

* **Neutral-colored clothing:** Avoid bright colors like neon yellow or red, as they can disturb the wildlife. Stick to greens, browns, and khakis.
* **A wide-brimmed hat:** Essential for sun protection.
* **A high-quality sports bra (for women):** The jeep tracks inside the parks are incredibly rugged!
* **Binoculars:** While your guide will point out animals, having your own pair of binoculars makes the experience much better (especially for spotting leopards in the trees).
